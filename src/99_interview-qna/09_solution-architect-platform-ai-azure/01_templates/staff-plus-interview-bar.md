# Staff / principal bar — Platform AI SA (why not trivia)

For **deep experience** (e.g. **20+ years** in industry), interviewers should **not** burn time on **SKU recall** or “name three Azure services.” They probe **judgment**: how you **standardize** without killing velocity, how you **govern** AI without blocking innovation, and how you **operate** **AKS** and **IaC** under **real** compliance pressure.

## What they are really scoring

| Signal | Weak answer | Strong answer |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Architecture** | Component laundry list | **Boundaries**, **failure domains**, **evolution** from MVP to global |
| **Trade-offs** | “Best practice” absolutes | **When you would flip** a choice; **cost / risk / speed** explicit |
| **Governance** | “We use Policy” | **Who decides exceptions**, **how drift is detected**, **audit** story |
| **AI platform** | One team owns everything | **Self-service** model, **guardrails**, **chargeback**, **shadow AI** prevention |
| **AKS** | “We run Kubernetes” | **Upgrade cadence**, **node pool strategy**, **network policy**, **identity** to PaaS |
| **Security** | Checklist | **Trust boundaries**, **blast radius**, **break-glass**, **supply chain** |
| **IaC / CI/CD** | “Terraform yes” | **Module ownership**, **env promotion**, **policy in pipeline**, **state** and **drift** |

## Interview format that surfaces depth fastest

1. **Whiteboard first** — 15–25 minutes per scenario; narrate **while** drawing.
2. **Troubleshooting** — 10 minutes: **hypotheses ordered**, **what you measure first**.
3. **Short Q&A** — only to **stress-test** what you drew.
