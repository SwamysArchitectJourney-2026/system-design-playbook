# Agentic AI lead: competency profile (hiring signals)

*Themes distilled from senior "agentic AI" and AI platform lead postings.*

## Programming and stack

- **Python** as the default implementation language for agents, tools, and glue.
- **Agent frameworks** — hands-on experience with **LangChain**, **LangGraph**, or comparable orchestration.
- **RAG** — End-to-end pipelines: chunking, embeddings, retrieval, evaluation—not only "we called an API."

## Product patterns

- **Chatbots and assistants** in production settings.
- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — servers, tool registration, secure invocation (increasingly explicit in job text).

## Cloud and models

- **AWS** focus in some roles: **Bedrock**, voice or multimodal services where listed.
- Ability to **deploy and operate** AI workloads (networking, IAM, scaling, cost).

## Leadership

- **Team lead** expectations: mentoring, roadmap alignment with product, cross-functional delivery.

## Nice-to-have extensions

- **Vector databases** (FAISS, Pinecone, OpenSearch, etc.).
- **Voice AI** pipelines.
- **Cloud-native** packaging (containers, Kubernetes) for inference and workers.

## How to prepare stories

Prepare **three** crisp narratives:

1. **One RAG system** — data flow, failure modes, evaluation metric.
2. **One agent** — tools, guardrails, state, human escalation.
3. **One MCP or tool-integration** — auth boundary, least privilege, logging.

Employers are screening for **shipping** agentic systems, not slide-deck familiarity.
