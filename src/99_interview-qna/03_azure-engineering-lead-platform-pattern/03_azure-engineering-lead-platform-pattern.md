# Azure engineering lead: platform and people pattern

*Typical signals from "engineering lead" or "platform lead" style Azure roles—mid-to-senior leadership band.*

## Experience and scope

Posts often target **roughly ten to fourteen years** total, with a strong **Azure delivery** track and **team leadership** (mentoring, hiring support, delivery governance).

## Technical bar

- **Azure** — AKS, App Services, Functions, integration and data services (exact mix varies by employer).
- **Cloud-native** — Distributed systems thinking, resilience, scaling.
- **DevOps** — CI/CD, **IaC** (Terraform, Bicep, or ARM), environment promotion.
- **APIs and microservices** — Service boundaries, contracts, versioning.
- **Containers** — Docker and Kubernetes operations at least at design-review depth.
- **Observability** — Monitoring, logging, tracing, SLO mindset.

## What you "do" day to day (as described by employers)

- Lead **application and integration** delivery on Azure.
- **Coach** engineers; raise quality and consistency.
- Own **architecture, security, and scalability** outcomes with stakeholders.
- Enforce **governance** (standards, approvals) while shipping.

## Interview angle

Be ready to alternate between **system design** (multi-service Azure layout) and **behavioral** stories: conflict on technical debt, timeline pressure, incident postmortems, and growing engineers.

## Skill stack shorthand for study lists

1. Draw a reference **landing zone** (network, identity, subscriptions).
2. Explain one **AKS** or **App Service** production deployment end to end.
3. Own a **CI/CD** story with secrets, environments, and rollback.
4. Speak to **cost and reliability** trade-offs with numbers or heuristics.
